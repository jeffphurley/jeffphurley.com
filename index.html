Career Narrative

My professional life started when I typed my very first resume and hand-delivered it to the head of HR at National Amusements. Well, not actually to her. I'd overestimated the power of simply knowing her name and asking to see her with nothing stronger than "but I brought a resume." I had grown up sneaking into their theatres almost weekly for any movie I could. I was a wayward young man, and published film critic, who determined National Amusements — owned by Sumner Redstone, chairman of Viacom, parent of Paramount, MTV, and CBS — was my shot at working inside the film industry without moving to New York or LA.

The person who can sit between the people who know what they need and the people who build the thing is more valuable than either side alone.

A 3-year detour into finance and organizational project management at a Boston-based investment bank came first. The last project was Y2K compliance — translating requirements between Accounting, Client Services, and IT against federal regulations. It taught me something I've used in every role since.

From my first day at National Amusements to my last day at Boston Globe Media, there wasn't a film released in the United States that I didn't touch some part of its marketing. Weekly campaigns for every major studio, every week, built on real-time weekend audience data. I became Manager of Domestic Advertising, negotiating 50+ annual media contracts worth over $70 million.

At Boston Globe Media I spent 12 years managing relationships with every major film studio, every Boston professional sports franchise, and live entertainment clients from Live Nation to Broadway Across America. I built programs that didn't exist before I built them. The studio advertising business I managed totaled $9.8 million in contracted annual spend across eight major studios in 2005 alone.

At National CineMedia the real product wasn't the screen — it was first-party data built from ticket purchases, loyalty behavior, mobile geo-location, and beacon networks across 20,000+ screens and 769 million annual attendees. Grew a territory from scratch: 30% revenue growth, 60%+ close rate.

At Threatpost I inherited a media platform with no commercial infrastructure — no product portfolio, no pricing, no sales process, no client relationships. Built all of it. Grew average contract value 7x. Beat revenue targets by 22%.

Product Innovations & First-Evers

First at BGM · 2004
First Fully Integrated Multi-Channel Program
Print + digital + broadcast + experiential executed simultaneously by a single team for a single client. Previously siloed by product line across all of BGM. Became organization-wide standard.
First at BGM · 2010 · MICA Award
First Editorial-Sponsored Content
BGM editorial staff working directly with advertising on a revenue-generating client campaign (Focus Features / Love Letters). Initially challenged internally. Later became industry standard.
First Known Precedent · 2007
Simpsons Movie Trading Cards — Studio IP Access
First known instance of a major Hollywood studio granting a regional newspaper publisher's creative team the right to render officially licensed characters for a physical collectible. Fox funded the production; BGM cost center inverted.
Proposed 2004–2005 · Anticipated Amazon Prime
Globe DVD Distribution Model
Formal proposal to distribute full-length studio films on DVDs in 700,000+ Sunday Globe newspapers. Content subscription + physical delivery hybrid conceived before Netflix pivoted to streaming. Machine testing began before organizational disruption ended the project.
Proposed 2004
Boston.com Virtual Shopping District
Subscriber delivery + digital commerce hybrid for Boston.com. Anticipated e-commerce bundled with local delivery infrastructure — a concept now foundational to same-day retail.
Proposed 2010
BGM Delivery Network Logistics Venture
Proposed monetizing existing Globe truck fleet and driver infrastructure for third-party delivery — a commercial logistics business built on existing BGM assets, years before gig economy delivery networks.
Across 12 Years at BGM
Only Fully Cross-Platform Sales Team at BGM
The sole team selling across all BGM products simultaneously while all other divisions remained product-siloed. Built and ran the model that eventually became the organizational standard.
July 2010
3D Newspaper Advertising Proposal
Proposed 3D advertising integration connecting the emerging 3D cinema trend to print advertising innovation following Verizon's 3D print campaign. Connecting the theatrical moment to the medium.
