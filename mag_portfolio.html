Most Consistent Revenue Producer · Sponsored Content & Co-Op Programs

Travel Series

4 Seasonal Issues Per Year · Most Consistently Positive Revenue Among All Globe Magazine Editions

Jeff led team sales into the full calendar of the weekly Globe Magazine with particular focus on the four seasonal Travel-themed issues — Spring Travel/Golf (Mar), Summer Travel (May), Fall Travel (Sep), and Winter Travel (Nov). Client demand and revenue growth led to adding a Weekend Fun Guide (Jun, 2012) — generating new revenue with further growth over 10% in 2013. The most reliably strong revenue growth strategy, new theme issues were added in 2013: Arts, Weddings, Staycations, Food (×2), Dining Out (×2), Music Gift Guide.

Beyond standard ad sales, sponsored content features and destination co-operative advertising programs were developed that materially expanded revenue by bringing in advertisers with no prior relationship with the section — regional tourism boards, destination hotels, airline partners, and travel brands pooling budgets into co-op units alongside editorial. Frequency discount programs locked multi-insertion annual commitments. These tactics became the model applied to the Arts Edition, Weekend Fun Guide, and every other new themed issue launched.

The Fall Travel Magazine alone drove 31 advertisers and 7 full-page ads in Q3 2011 with a 5% revenue gain over the prior year — while the broader travel category delivered 38% year-over-year digital growth the same period.

Summer Arts Weekend

Copley Plaza Hub · July 2012 (Inaugural) · Co-Produced with WGBH · Presented by Citizens Bank

A multiday citywide festival launched in 2012 by Boston Globe Media and public media powerhouse WGBH — Jeff led BGM revenue, collaborating with WGBH to structure, close, and execute sponsorships and media integrations, while managing design, content, production, and distribution of the printed program guide.

Jeff worked closely between sales, customers, and internal stakeholders at two separate presenting organizations — structuring the sponsorship tier architecture, managing a 100+ account pipeline, negotiating institutional contracts, and overseeing on-site client activations including sponsor booths, stage signage, hospitality tents, and video displays throughout the four-day event's Copley Plaza hub.

500,000 copies of the Program Guide were the custom publishing anchor of Summer Arts Weekend: home-delivered in the Sunday Globe and Worcester T&G, direct mailed to 110,000 WGBH members, and distributed on-site, it featured full-page advertising from brand sponsors, arts institutions, and destination advertisers — each sold by Jeff's Arts and Travel teams as part of the broader sponsorship packages.

The Guide was supported by a digital event microsite on Boston.com, a mobile app, Boston.com TicketWatch email promotions to 60,000+ subscribers, 160 radio spots on WGBH 89.7 and 99.5 Classical, and 160 TV spots on WGBH Channel 2. Revenue spanned multiple streams: tiered cash sponsorships, in-guide advertising, on-site booth fees, and trade arrangements with arts institution participants. The event continued three additional summers, validating the model.

"G" Section — Daily Arts & Entertainment Magazine

The "G" Section was Boston Globe Media's daily magazine-format product — a Monday–Saturday arts and entertainment section that Jeff Hurley helped develop in collaboration with editorial and then led the full client roll-out as a new magazine-style daily content format.

Product Development · Client Roll-Out · Premium Formats · 229,900+ Weekly Readers

"G" Section — Magazine Format Development & Launch

Monday–Saturday · Daily Arts & Entertainment · New Magazine-Style Format

Jeff collaborated with editorial on the development of the "G" Section as a new daily magazine-style content format — then led the full client roll-out, bringing advertisers into a redesigned product with new premium positions and a stronger editorial identity. The transition from a traditional section to a magazine-style daily was a significant commercial proposition that required product education, new creative format development, and coordinated go-to-market execution across sales, editorial, production, and marketing.

A suite of premium advertising products was developed and sold specifically for the magazine format: Pop-Out tab extensions, Gatefold wraps, Post-Oscar cover takeovers (a magazine-style cover format created specifically for Oscars Monday), Love Week editorial packages, and the annual Plonkapalooza wine guide. Each format was positioned as incremental to standard ROP — generating premium revenue from existing accounts while opening the door to new advertiser categories drawn to the magazine aesthetic.

"G" Section readers indexed above market for arts attendance, fine dining, museum visits, and cultural engagement — making it the natural advertising home for entertainment studios, performing arts organizations, restaurants, and lifestyle advertisers.

Jeff also authored client education, reservation materials, and spec guides for the Globe's two most premium front-page formats. The Spadea — an unbound broadsheet wrapping the paper's front page — required 6–8 week lead times and a custom reservation process. The G-Section Gatefold offered an upgraded paper stock four-panel spread across the inside front cover. Both were positioned as first-in-market opportunities with limited annual availability, creating genuine scarcity that supported premium pricing and enabled competitive displacement from rival publications.
